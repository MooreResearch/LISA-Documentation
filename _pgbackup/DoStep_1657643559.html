DoStep

MethodEvolverEvolveretc.

Calculates values of \( v \), \( \partial v \over \partial \delta \), \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{1 \ell} \), and \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{2 \ell} \) at a time \(d \tau \) in the future using the leapfrog method.

Technical description:
This code uses past and present values of \( v \), \( \partial v \over \partial \delta \), \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{1 \ell} \), and \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{2 \ell} \) to calculate their future values using the leapfrog method. The derivatives of \( v \), \( \partial v \over \partial \delta \), \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{1 \ell} \), and \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{2 \ell} \) with respect to a dimensionless time \(\tau_s = \frac{t_s}{M} \) are found using the EquationSolver class which contains analytic solutions to these derivatives. The leapfrog method is then used to calculate future values of each derivative. This method updates the past and present value of each derivative, setting what used to be the present value to the past value, and the recently calculated future value as the present value.

Theoretical description:
This method calculates \( v \), \( \partial v \over \partial \delta \), \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{1 \ell} \), and \( \partial v \over \partial \chi_{2 \ell} \) at a time step \(d \tau\) in the future by using the leapfrog method:
 \( x_{future} =  x_{past} + 2\Delta \tau \dot{x}_{present}\)
Where \( \dot{x} \) is the derivative of x with respect to dimensionless time \( \tau_s = \frac{t_s}{M} in the source frame. See Equation (1.12) of the Theory1 document for more information about the leapfrog method.
