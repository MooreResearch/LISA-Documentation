SpinEvolver Constructor

MethodSpin CaseSpinEvolver

This method initailizes the first two values of \(\iota\), \(\alpha\), \(\hat{L}_N\), \( \hat{\chi}_1 \), and \( \hat{\chi}_2 \). It also calculates the first value of \(\dot{\alpha}\) and calculates a suggested ideal time step based on the rate of change of \(\hat{L}_N\), \( \hat{\chi}_1 \), and \( \hat{\chi}_2 \).

Technical description:
First this method sets the values of necessary constants, as well as \(\delta\) and \(\eta\). Then the intial values of \(\iota\), \(\alpha\), and \(\hat{L}_N\) are computed using Equations (1.32a,b) and Equation (1.7) respectively. Then, \(\hat{\chi}_1\) and \(\hat{\chi}_2\) are defined and used by the GetLNhatdot method to obtain \(\dot{\hat{L}}_N\) which is used in Euler's method to obtain the "Future" value of \(\hat{L}_N\). Equations (1.32a,b) are then used again to obtain the "Future" values for \(\iota\) and \(\alpha\) from \(\hat{L}_N\). Then, the initial value of \(\dot{\alpha}\) is calcuted, and finally \( d \hat{\chi}_1 \over d \tau \) and \( d \hat{\chi}_2 \over d \tau \) are computed and used in Euler's method to find the "Future" values of \(\hat{\chi}_1\) and \(\hat{\chi}_2\). At the end of the method, a suggested ideal time step is calculated based on the rate of change of \(\hat{L}_N\), \( \hat{\chi}_1 \), and \( \hat{\chi}_2 \).
Parameter Type Description
v0 Double The initial value of the post newtionian parameter \(v = (M\omega)^{1/3} \)
dτ0 Double The initial value of a small step in unitless time \(\tau = \frac{t_s}{M} \) where \(t_s\) is time in the source frame.
myδ Double \( \delta = \frac{ m_1 - m_2 }{m_1 + m_2 } \) a unitless measure of the mass difference between the two stars.
Ln0 Double The initial value of of the z component of \(\hat{L}_N\)
myι0 Double The inital angle between our line-of-sight and the binary orbital angular momentum.
myα0 Double The initial angle between the projection of the system's orbital angular momentum in the xy plane, and the x axis.
χ1x Double The x component of star 1's angular momentum \(\vec{\chi}_1 \)
χ1y Double The y component of star 1's angular momentum \(\vec{\chi}_1 \)
χ1z Double The z component of star 1's angular momentum \(\vec{\chi}_1 \)
χ2x Double The x component of star 2's angular momentum \(\vec{\chi}_2 \)
χ2y Double The y component of star 2's angular momentum \(\vec{\chi}_2 \)
χ2z Double The z component of star 2's angular momentum \(\vec{\chi}_2 \)
